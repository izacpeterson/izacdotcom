Conway's Game of Life

The Game of Life, created by John Conway in 1970, is a zero-player, celullar automaton game with 4 simple rules, listed below. This example populates the gird with random living cells, which 'live' and 'die' as the game progresses.

  1. 1. Any live cell with two or three neighbors survives to the next generation.
  2. 2. Any live cell with fewer than two neighbors dies.
  3. 3. Any live cell with more than three neighbors dies.
  4. 4. Any dead cell with exactly three neighbors becomes a live cell.
